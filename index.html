La Comtesse d'Escarbagna

This play by Molière, La Comtesse d'Escarbagnas, is one of his least known works. It wasn't published during Molière life time and after reading it you will understand why. It certainly doesn't measure up to the other works that are still being presented today.

This play was chosen for this project as a means of demonstrating that the creating of interlinear text and syncing it with audio can be done using readily available open source tools.

The play met serval criteria:

  • All the elements had to either be in the public domain or relased under the proper use licenses.
  • An audio recording of decent quality.
  • The text needed to be in a useable format.
  • The text and audio had to agree in order for the syncing to work.
  • And it should be a good length, between 25 to 35 minutes.
  • A bonus is that it is by Molière who has great name recogination.

Translation

The play is machine translated using the Google Chrome browser. Yup, not nearly as good as a human translation would have been, but it is fast.

This is a major trade off and the where I get the most negatives reactions. So for all groaners I have the solution; which is to crowd souce a better translation.

Here is a list of the scenes which link to a Google Spread sheet. When you see a tranlation that can be improve, go to the spreadsheet; find the word or phase and enter it into column C. You can leave a comment explaining your the reason. When you make an edit and/or comment the page editor will be notified.

End Note

This site is the first part of providing evidence that an interlinear site can be built with open source or free resources. Right now this is a one person shop, but it's hoped that others will see the value in this approach and step in. The one person running this shop is only interested in French as a target language and someone else needs to do other language pairs.

The best combination of language pairs will most likely have English as a target language. Someone should step up and take charge. That the major reason this work is being done is to show that it can be done. Hopefully this will start a community to support the work of JHS.
